# Sources

Personal notes on what this skill was built from. Not copied when the skill is
imported into a project — this is a memory-jog for the author, not part of the skill.

The skill distills these sources; it does not reproduce them. Follow the links /
titles below to the originals.

| Source | What it contributed |
|---|---|
| Chris Andrews, *Character and Structure: The Foundations of Fiction* (ISBN 978-1925803068) | Audit categories 1 & 3: **missing/toothless rules** and **unbounded power** — rules must generate conflict and powers must have limits/costs, else the story resolves itself. |
| Michelle Barker, "Building a Fictional World: A Novelist's Guide" — darlingaxe.com/blogs/news/world-building | Category 2 (coherence: weather↔crops↔food↔clothing, history↔government); category 4 (**the world eating the story** / over-building); category 5 (infodump risk, one-new-thing, direwolf landmark). |
| Will Storr, *The Science of Storytelling* (ISBN 978-1419743030) | Category 6: **thin/abstract rendering** — abstract adjectives vs. ~3 concrete sensory qualities; why detail is required for the reader to build the world. |
| Janet Burroway, *Writing Fiction: A Guide to Narrative Craft* (ISBN 978-0226616698) | Category 6, the emotional angle: setting must reflect or resist the viewpoint character's mood, not sit as neutral backdrop. |
| Ross Hartmann, *The Structure of Story* (ISBN 978-1735603827) | Background for the constructive counterpart (`/world-building`); the audit checks that a world built by that method holds together (beings, objects, value systems present and consistent). |

This skill mirrors the findings-table format of `swe/code-smell-audit` and pairs
with `writing/world-building` as its critique counterpart.
